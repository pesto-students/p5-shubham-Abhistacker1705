India,Geopolitics and Business

The geopolitics of India must be considered in the geographical context of the Indian subcontinent — a self-contained region that includes India, Pakistan, Bangladesh and, depending how one defines it, Nepal and Bhutan. We call the subcontinent "self-contained" because it is a region that is isolated on all sides by difficult terrain or by ocean. In geopolitical terms it is, in effect, an island..

African countries offer India lithium access to repay loans.

India over the years have been giving soft loans to African countries,which is more than 12.3 billion dollars. Also special developmental projects are done in some African countries like ,National Assembly building in Gambia ,Hydropower projects in Zambia etc.

Africa due to current pandemic is in crisis hence can't repay the loan but African countries offered Lithium resources in Africa to be extracted by Indian companies.

This will be a huge deal for Indian EV industry also will be huge opportunity for African workforce.

India will stop using Petrol in 5 years - Minister of Road Transport Nitin Gadkari.

Minister of Road Transport and Highways, Nitin Gadkari recently stated that Petrol vehicles will vanish from roads in next 5 years

Why this is important for India?

Fuel Import Bill of India is almost $200 billion. India recently increased ethanol blending in fuel to 10% and will gradually increase to 20% by 2025. This move has approximately had an impact of 42000cr in forex of India. Also benifits sugarcane farmers as an additional source of income.

How this can be achieved?

India is spending huge resources on research of hydrogen fuel cells an has partnered with several European countries for research. Also flex fuel engines, which enable high % ethanol blended fuels to run are also being considered.
